If a bookmaker offers you 2.50 odds on a team winning, they're essentially saying "for every $1 you bet, you get $2.50 back if you win."
Flipping that around — if the payout is 2.50x your money, the bookmaker is implying the team has a 1 in 2.50 chance of winning, which is 40%.

total = sum(raw_probs.values())
vig = (total - 1) * 100

Adds up all the implied probabilities. 
As we know they sum to something like 1.043 instead of 1.0. 
Subtracting 1 and multiplying by 100 converts that excess into a readable percentage, so 1.043 becomes 4.3% vig

scipy.special.expit = sigmoid function, i.e.  σ(x)=1/(1+(e^(−x))) => convert something that lives on an infinite scale into a probability that has to stay between 0 and 1.
The 0.15 is a scaling factor that compresses the point difference before feeding it into sigmoid, because without it sigmoid would be too aggressive; a +5 net rating difference would give 99% win probability which is unrealistic for basketball. It was chosen manually to map typical NBA point differences into a sensible probability range of roughly 20-80%, though a more advanced model would learn this number from the data automatically.
